What the Hell Is Kloppy?

A real desktop assistant wearing a deeply unserious paperclip.

Kloppy is a free, open-source desktop assistant for local notes, reminders, folder commentary, and chat with a small AI model that runs on your computer.

He lives in the system tray, stores your data locally, and does not require an account, subscription, or cloud service. It is useful software. The labor dispute is mostly decorative.

The paperwork

  • MIT-licensed source you can inspect
  • Local JSON storage in Electron user data

FAQ

Frequently attempted excuses and several useful answers.

Which systems are actually supported?

The published v0.1.0 artifacts are Windows x64, macOS on Intel and Apple Silicon through one universal build, and Linux x86_64/amd64 through AppImage or .deb. No Windows ARM, Linux ARM, Android, iOS, or web application artifact has been published, so those buttons do not exist.

Why does my operating system warn me?

Kloppy v0.1.0 is legitimately unsigned and macOS is not notarized. Use the exact SmartScreen or Gatekeeper instructions on the download card, and compare the displayed SHA-256 with the checksum file on the release page if you want to verify the installer.

Is he spyware?

No. There is no account or telemetry. App data stays on your machine, folder watching is opt-in, and chat talks only to a local llamafile. The optional model download is the one external app request and starts only after you approve it.

Does the $4.20 buy the application?

No. The application is free and the contribution unlocks nothing. It is a voluntary one-time tip for future improvements and a legally questionable amount of closure.

What does the optional payment send?

Nothing goes to Stripe until you open a contribution link. Stripe then hosts the checkout and collects the contact, payment, and tax information it displays. The link includes fixed campaign labels, not a visitor ID or Certificate of Forgiveness name. Kloppy’s site has no analytics script.

What happens when I close the window?

Kloppy hides in the system tray instead of quitting. Use the tray menu to show him, hide him, summon a popup, or actually quit. He has been briefed on the final option.

Does the local AI work offline?

After you explicitly download the recommended checksum-verified llamafile—or choose a compatible one already on your machine—chat runs locally on 127.0.0.1. Notes and reminders also work without cloud services.

Why does the repository have more than 69 commits?

The historical claim refers to the original Kloppy v0.1.0 app-and-launch state, which landed at exactly 69 repository commits. Later website work improved the download experience, launch messaging, and Certificate of Forgiveness. Sixty-nine is a milestone, not a permanent commit-count ceiling.
